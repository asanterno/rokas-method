// The System
"Training is a system, not a mood. Connection is the skill. Strength is the outcome."
01
Body Awareness
Feel every rep. Slow down to speed up. Kettlebells don't let you fake it.
02
Strength + Mobility
They are not separate. Build strength in every position, not just the easy ones.
03
Breathwork
Your breath is your foundation — for focus, endurance, recovery, and performance.
04
Intention
Every set has a purpose. Drive from the ground. Control the descent. Own the position.

// Breathwork — Rokas Method
Breathing is your number one priority. It improves focus, sleep, endurance, and performance.
4–4–4–4
Box Breathing
Inhale 4s → Hold 4s → Exhale 4s → Hold 4s. Activates the parasympathetic system. Builds CO₂ tolerance and focus.
↳ Pre-workout, 3–5 min
Nasal Only
Nasal Breathing During Training
Stay nasal as long as possible during sets. When you break to mouth breathing, rest. Trains efficiency and aerobic capacity over time.
↳ All sessions — especially flow days
4–6
Recovery Breathing
Inhale 4s through the nose, exhale 6s through pursed lips. Longer exhale activates the vagus nerve and speeds recovery between heavy sets.
↳ Between heavy sets (D1 & D2)
5 min
Daily Morning Practice
5 minutes of slow nasal breathing upon waking. Inhale 5s, exhale 5s. Sets your nervous system tone for the entire day.
↳ Every morning, non-negotiable
// Nutrition Framework
🥩
Protein First
  • Target 2g protein per kg of bodyweight daily
  • Prioritize whole sources: eggs, meat, fish, Greek yoghurt
  • Eat protein within 1 hour of training
  • Every meal anchored by a protein source
⚡
Fuel Smart
  • Eat complex carbs on training days (oats, rice, potatoes)
  • Pre-workout meal 60–90 min before training
  • Post-workout: protein + carbs within 45 min
  • Reduce carbs on rest days — prioritise vegetables
💧
Hydration
  • Minimum 2.5–3L of water daily
  • Start every morning with 500ml before coffee
  • Add electrolytes (sodium, magnesium) on training days
  • Limit caffeine after 2pm for sleep quality
🌙
Recovery Habits
  • 7–9 hours of sleep is a non-negotiable training variable
  • Avoid screens 60 min before bed
  • Walk 20–30 min on rest days — low intensity, nasal breathing
  • Limit alcohol — it directly impairs recovery and adaptation
